Conservation

Protecting the
local landscape

Essex Gardens Trust works to protect historic parks, gardens & designed landscapes through research, recording & responding to planning proposals.


EGT provides informed comment when gardens or designed landscapes are implicated by contemporary development. This requires us to comment on national Government development policy, Local Development Framework plans, on local planning applications, or pre-application stage proposals.


The work of EGT's Garden History team is vital to its role in contemporary Planning & Development. The detailed inventories it prepares of every Essex district help to identify important heritage sites across the county. Many have importance at only a local level and therefore may not be protected by the National Heritage list.

EGT Conservation Team

Our Conservation Team works hard to produce inventories & garden research to help protect our local greenspaces & educate everyone about the importance of these spaces.

They are also responsible for responding to planning applications & taking action when considered necessary based on their research and existing knowledge.

The team are currently monitoring various regional projects, including the National Grid's plans for a line of pylons running close to Rivenhall Place, a location previously inventoried by the trust.

Work with us

Contact the conservation team if you are concerned about a local planning application or would like some advice.
